About Department

Department of Textile Technology, PSG Polytechnic College has its origin as LTM during 1944. After few years it was revived as Diploma in Textile Technology in the year 1977 and in the same year PSG Polytechnic College had its autonomy. The sandwich programme in this field was started during the year 1983.

The course was planned with the main objective as to equip the students mainly to handle shop floor management as supervisors and to solve day to day technical problems in Textile Mills.

The curriculum imparts both theoretical and practical knowledge to the students in core areas like Spinning, Weaving, Knitting, Chemical Processing, Garment Manufacturing, Textile Testing and Textile CAD.

Apart from their theoretical input, the students will undergo weekly industrial visits and six weeks advanced industrial training during their vacation period. This will facilitate them to get exposed to practical working in textile industry. During their final year of this programme, students will carry out a project work related to problem solving in Textile industry.

Course Structure

CodeProgramme Outcomes
PO1Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and textile engineering fundamentals to solve textile processing and manufacturing problems.
PO2Problem analysis: Identify and analyse well-defined textile engineering problems using standard methods in spinning, weaving, processing and garmenting.
PO3Design/development of solutions: Design solutions for textile manufacturing processes, fabric structures and quality control systems.
PO4Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern textile testing instruments, CAD software and appropriate techniques for fiber, yarn and fabric analysis.
PO5Engineering practices for society, sustainability: Apply sustainable practices in textile processing, waste management and ethical manufacturing.
PO6Project Management: Manage textile production projects individually or as a team member, effectively communicate in industrial environment.
PO7Life-long learning: Engage in continuous learning in emerging areas like technical textiles, smart textiles and automation.
CodeProgramme Specific Outcomes
PSO1Ability to manage shop floor operations in spinning, weaving, processing and garment manufacturing units with technical competence.
PSO2Apply modern textile technologies including CAD, automation and quality control systems to enhance productivity and product quality.
CodeProgramme Educational Objectives
PEO1To produce technically skilled diploma engineers capable of handling supervisory roles in textile and garment industries.
PEO2To develop entrepreneurial skills and innovative thinking for contributing to the growth of textile sector.
PEO3To prepare students for higher education and lifelong learning in emerging textile technologies.

Facilities

CHEMICAL PROCESSING LAB
The Textile Chemical Processing Laboratory comprises of wide range of equipments for processing fibers, yarns, fabrics and garments. Salient features: Padding Mangle, Digital Oven Chamber, Weighting Balance, Magnetic Stirrer, Stainless Steel rectangular water bath. The laboratory has varieties of natural dyes and mordant chemicals.

Laboratory In charge : Dr. B. Sathish Babu
TEXTILE TESTING LABORATORY
The Textile Testing Laboratory is equipped with the entire range of testing equipments which include sophisticated instruments like Microprocessor based Twist Tester and Electronic Evenness Tester with Spectrogram. It also possesses Electronic Tensile Strength Tester from Mesdan, Italy to determine the tensile characteristics of yarn and fabric.

Laboratory In charge : Ms. Shri Pradha
SPINNING & KNITTING LABORATORY
The Spinning Laboratory is bestowed with complete range of machinery, which includes the carding, speed frame, ring frame, comber and cone winding. Knitting section features circular knitting machines and flat knitting equipment.

Laboratory In charge : Dr. M. Senthilkumar
TEXTILE CAD CENTRE
The department owns the pride of possessing the Textile CAD Centre with the latest software's on woven design technology and Apparel design technology. The facilities include Pentium systems, Scanners, Plotters, and software’s such as Win Cad Vantage, TCS Jacquard and TCS Dobby, Textronics.

Laboratory In charge : Dr. B. Sathish Babu
